The day before the tournament ...

  • PARKING: Print this and put it on the dashboard of your vehicle.
  • DRESS CODE:
  • Please dress appropriately -- this is a tournament in a college!
  • Wear short sleeve shirts and shorts (women may wear skirts) with the color not matching the ball’s color -- white. If you want to annoy your opponent, wear pure white. But we will not let you play!
  • Wear athletic/sports shoes with rubber soles. High heels, slippers, bare feet, stilts, etc might lead to injuries.
  • No hats, but head bands are fine. Just make sure it’s not white.
  • DIRECTIONS: http://cctt0.pythonanywhere.com/direction. If you have problems, email yliow@ccis.edu.
  • WHAT TO BRING:
  • Towel, a bottle of water, snacks, change of shirt, etc.
  • Your racket must be black on one side, red on the other. Don’t have a racket? Don’t panic! I’ll bring a couple. There is also a small sale of rackets at the tournaments.
  • Sandpaper racket is illegal! Nittaku balls are expensive! Let’s not destroy them!
  • Cash for tournament fees and possibly lunch/dinner
  • Cash is best and exact amount if possible because I don't have a staff -- I’m the only person running this thing!
  • Pay me during practice or when you check-in at your first tournament. (I’m usually extremely busy during the tournaments.)
  • 100% of proceeds go to the club. Donations appreciated. The club cannot survive without money and we have big plans for the club.

At the tournament(s)  ...

  • There’s free breakfast on Saturday at 8AM -- 2 doz bagels and coffee
  • ARRIVAL:
  • Plan to arrive 30 minutes early, i.e., be ready to play your matches about 30 minutes before the stated schedule. There are lots of variables in planning all the 224 matches (phew!) It’s extremely difficult to nail down the exact starting time of your matches. Plus if you are there early, you will probably find an unused table and warm up with someone. And you get to chat with old friends and make new ones -- which is the point of the tournament right?
  • There will be a check-in table. Pay the tournament fee at the table if you have not paid during the open play meetings.
  • I'll also take orders for lunch/dinner. We had Jimmy John's during the spring 2016 tournament; maybe we can do something else this time. Of course you can also bring your own lunch/dinner.
  • SCORES: Track the score at Scores.

MATCH TIMES:

  • Wait for your name to be announced. (Obviously we cannot wait indefinitely. If you’re overly late, your place might be taken up by someone else.)
  • I’ve allocated 15 minutes per match.
  • Make sure you (and your opponent) get the scoring sheet before the match. It’s perfectly OK to shake the hand of your opponent when you both arrive at the table.
  • Warm up before the match: 2 minutes max.
  • Record the points at the end of each game -- don't wait till the end of the whole match! You’ll forget!
  • There is no formal umpiring. So it's up to the players to keep track of the points. If you are watching, you are more than welcome to volunteer to help keep track of the points, watch for lets, etc.
  • At the end of the whole match, shake the hand of your opponent again (high five, fist bump, etc. are OK too.) and thank him/her for the match. I know many of us know one another, but there are newcomers/strangers – we don’t want them to think we’re rude. Shake the hand of the person who umpired the match (if there’s one). If the opponent has a coach -- shake his/her hand too. If in doubt -- shake everyone’s hand!
  • For round robin matches, winner takes unplayed games. For instance if you won a 5-game match with 11-2,11-3,11-4, then it will be recorded as 11-2,11-3,11-4,11-0, 11-0. (Those extra games might come in handy during tie breakers.)
  • Turn in the scoring sheet right away.

SPORTSMANSHIP

  • Please don’t shout, scream, curse loudly, throw your paddle, stomp on the balls, etc. We’ll have to ban you if you do! Cursing at yourself quietly is OK. (Go to youtube and search “angry table tennis” for some “interesting” videos.)
  • Don't gloat. There's always someone better than you.
  • AGAIN: Shake the hand of your opponent(s) after a match.

DOUBLES

  • If you don’t have a partner for doubles, don’t worry – we’ll find someone for you.
  • If your partner keeps losing points – relax. He/she is trying his/her best. Chill it. This is not part of the ITTF world tour and you're not losing $10k. Just relax and enjoy the tournament. (Again, cursing at yourself quietly is OK.)

ADVANCED SINGLES:

  • We will be more picky for the advanced group. Google for “table tennis rules” on your own.
  • The most important thing to check is probably service rules.
  • You can check the paddle of your opponent before the match.
  • You can have one 1-minute time out (if you wish) during a match.
  • If for health reason(s) you need to take longer breaks, just let us know. We will try our best to accommodate.

Things you probably do not want to read

Seeding and forming the RR and ER brackets

  • In general: We create a ranking of players and distribute them so that as much as possible, the best players meet as late as possible. For instance, say A, B, C, D are the top 4 players in a tournament and there are 4 RR groups. Then we put A,B,C,D into groups 1, 3, 2, 4. If A, B, C, D moved onto the ER, then A plays C and B plays D. Etc.
  • I will be asking the more advanced players that have been in the club for some time and have an overall understanding of the players to help with forming the RR groups.The finalized line up will be sent out 1 week before the tournament (maybe earlier depending on when the tournament is completely filled up). If you need to talk to us to recommend changes, do let me know.

Round robin ranking

This is how we’re going to calculate rankings in a RR in case there are ties. We’ll go “match, then games, then points, then head-to-head”, and then we really hope there are no more ties!

  1. [M] Ranking by match points: Players are awarded match points based matches: 2 points for a match won and 1 point for a match lost. (A player wins by default if his opponent does not show up “in time”.) For each group of players which are in the same match-point-rank, go on to the next step for tie breaking.
  2. [M] per group: For each group of players at the same rank in 1. above, go over [M] again but this time using only matches within the group. (If this group has 2 players, then it’s head-to-head.) Ties within this group go onto step 3.
  3. [G] Break tie with game points: Compute the ratios of wins-losses for games and each player in this group and rank them. If a player won 3-1 in 5-game match, then this is counted as 4-1. If a player won 4-1 in a 7-game match, then this is counted as 6-1. If player A requests to stop a game in a match against B -- example: injury or family emergency -- then B wins all the points remaining points in that game and gets 11-0 for all remaining unplayed games. For instance, say A was winning 11-1, 11-1 and in the third game A request to stop at 5-1, then the match is recorded as 11-1, 11-1, 5-11, 0-11, 0-11.
  4. [P] Break tie with points: Compute the ratio of win-losses for points for each player in the group.
  5. [H] Break tie with head-to-head: In case counting points is a tie, we will go head-to-head.
  6. If head-to-head is a tie, we draw lots.

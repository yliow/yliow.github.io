For Attendees!

Congratulations! You have registered for CCCS High School Programming Contest #9 (Spring 2025). The following is some information that will be helpful. The goal of the programming contest is simple: learn, make friends, and have fun.

Preparation

  • Here’s our CCCS HS Programming Contest discord server (https://discord.gg/EVjhfQRx). You can access this using your browser, but it’s probably best to download the discord app. Several of us are at the discord server. Feel free to introduce yourself and chat with us and with each other. Let us know if you have questions.
  • If you have been studying C++/Python/Java, then you’re ready. Of course the more you practice the better you get. In the future, we might expand to include other languages.
  • Review your old assignments from your programming classes. Obviously your instructor and your classmates would be helpful when you review and continue to study programming, problem solving, and computer science.
  • If you run out of problems (from your textbooks and from our instructors), no problem. Go to our discord server and ask for more problems.
  • We encourage you to bring your own laptop (and charger). There are PCs and laptops at Buchanan Hall. But you are always more comfortable and efficient with your own laptop.
  • Click here to go to the contest rules. We will go over the rules on the day of the contest.

Prizes

For Spring 2025, at this point (3/9/2025), the prizes are approximately
  • Tuition scholarship prizes: 3 x $1000
  • Cash prizes: $800 (probably in Amazon cash/gift cards)
Some restriction apply for the tuition scholarship prizes. Details (and other scholarship information) will be provided during the event.

We hope to grow the event in terms of the number of attendees and the total prize amount every year.

On the day

Of course the important thing is to get to the welcome/check-in/registration table! Once you’re there, the counselors (my CS majors) will guide you for the rest of the day.
  • You are strongly encouraged to bring your laptop and charger if possible. There will be PCs and laptops at the contest venue.
  • Bring cash for t-shirt. Venmo is OK too.
  • Address: Columbia College at Columbia MO, 1001 Rogers St, Columbia, MO 65216 (google map)
  • Parking: For event attendees, parking is free! No event hangtag, no sign-on-dashboard, etc. The 10th Street parking lot is the most convenient. But you can use any non-residential parking lot.
    • pdf map: look for "A. 10th Street".
    • or interactive map: under "Parking Lots", look for "Tenth Street Lot".
  • Once parked, head toward the contest venue, Buchanan Hall. The check-in room is room 103.
    • pdf map: look for "16. Buchanan Hall".
    • or interactive map: under "Buildings", look for "Buchanan Hall".
  • The welcome/check-in/registration table in Buchanan Hall (room 103) will open at 8AM. Opening remarks begin at ~8:30AM. Breakfast is provided! Let us know if you will be late. If you are lost message, message us in discord or send us an email.
